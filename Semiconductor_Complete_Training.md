## Introduction
This training document aims to provide a comprehensive overview of integrated semiconductor technologies, focusing on both the technical processes involved in semiconductor manufacturing and the current landscape of the semiconductor industry.

## Semiconductor Basics
- **What is a Semiconductor?**  
  A semiconductor is a material that has electrical conductivity between a conductor and an insulator. It forms the foundation of modern electronic devices.
- **Types of Semiconductors:**  
  - Intrinsic Semiconductors  
  - Extrinsic Semiconductors

## Technical Process Flow
### Wafer Fabrication
The first step in semiconductor manufacturing involves the creation of wafers from silicon or other semiconductor materials.

### Doping Process
Doping introduces impurities into the semiconductor to modify its electrical properties.  
- **N-type Doping:** Adds electrons  
- **P-type Doping:** Creates holes

### Lithography
This process involves transferring a pattern onto the wafer using light.

### Etching
Etching removes layers from the surface of the wafer to create patterns.

### Deposition
Deposition processes, such as chemical vapor deposition (CVD), add materials to the wafer surface.

### Packaging
The final step where the semiconductor chips are installed into protective packages and connected to external circuits.
